# Lesson overview

This lesson covers essential Japanese language skills for dealing with accommodation in Japan. You'll learn how to make and modify reservations, check in and out of hotels and ryokans, report problems with your room or facilities, and ask for help with transportation. The lesson emphasizes polite, formal language appropriate for customer service interactions, and includes practical dialogues for real-world situations you'll encounter while traveling in Japan.

# Preparation

## Grammar Explanation

### Polite Request Forms

When dealing with accommodation staff, you'll frequently need to make polite requests. Here are the key patterns:

**~たいのですが (I would like to...)**
- Used to express desires politely
- Example: 予約をしたいのですが (I would like to make a reservation)
- The のですが ending softens the request and invites a response

**~をお願いします / ~をお願いしたいのですが (Please... / I'd like to request...)**
- Direct but polite request form
- Example: チェックインをお願いします (Check-in, please)
- Adding したいのですが makes it even more indirect and polite

**~ていただけますか (Could you...?)**
- Very polite request form, literally "could I receive the favor of you doing...?"
- Example: 部屋を変えていただけますか (Could you change my room?)
- This is one of the most respectful ways to ask for something

**~てください (Please do...)**
- Standard polite request
- Example: 教えてください (Please tell me)
- Less formal than ていただけますか but still polite

### Honorific Prefixes: お and ご

In hotel and service contexts, many nouns take the honorific prefix お or ご:
- お部屋 (room), お名前 (name), お電話 (phone)
- ご予約 (reservation), ご住所 (address), ご利用 (use/service)
- Generally, ご is used with words of Chinese origin (音読み), お with native Japanese words (訓読み)

### Problem Reporting Pattern: ~が + State/Problem

When reporting issues:
- Subject marker が identifies what has the problem
- Example: エアコンが壊れています (The AC is broken)
- Example: お湯が出ません (Hot water isn't coming out)

### Duration Counters

- 泊 (はく/ぱく): counter for nights stayed
  - 一泊 (いっぱく): one night
  - 二泊 (にはく): two nights
  - 三泊 (さんぱく): three nights
- 名 (めい): counter for people (polite)
  - 一名 (いちめい): one person
  - 二名 (にめい): two people

## Useful Phrases

<phrasebank>
- 予約をお願いしたいのですが: I'd like to make a reservation (予約 = "reservation"; を = object marker; お願いしたい = "would like to request"; のですが = explanatory soft ending that invites response)
- 二泊三日で予約したいです: I'd like to book for two nights, three days (二泊 = "two nights"; 三日 = "three days"; で = particle indicating scope/parameters; 予約したい = "want to reserve")
- チェックインをお願いします: I'd like to check in, please (チェックイン = "check-in"; を = object marker; お願いします = polite "please" or "I request")
- 予約の変更をお願いできますか: Could I request a change to my reservation? (予約の変更 = "reservation change"; を = object marker; お願いできますか = "can I request?", very polite form)
- 部屋を変えていただけますか: Could you change my room? (部屋 = "room"; を = object marker; 変えて = te-form of "to change"; いただけますか = "could I receive the favor?", extremely polite)
- エアコンが壊れています: The air conditioner is broken (エアコン = "air conditioner"; が = subject marker; 壊れています = "is broken", state resulting from action)
- お湯が出ません: Hot water isn't coming out (お湯 = "hot water" with honorific prefix; が = subject marker; 出ません = negative form of "to come out")
- タクシーを呼んでいただけますか: Could you call a taxi for me? (タクシー = "taxi"; を = object marker; 呼んで = te-form of "to call"; いただけますか = "could I receive the favor?")
- 駅までの行き方を教えてください: Please tell me how to get to the station (駅 = "station"; まで = "to/until"; の = possessive/connecting particle; 行き方 = "way of going/how to get"; を = object marker; 教えてください = "please teach/tell")
- 朝食は何時からですか: What time does breakfast start? (朝食 = "breakfast"; は = topic marker; 何時 = "what time"; から = "from"; です = copula; か = question marker)
- チェックアウトは何時までですか: What time is check-out until? (チェックアウト = "check-out"; は = topic marker; 何時 = "what time"; まで = "until"; です = copula; か = question marker)
- 荷物を預かっていただけますか: Could you hold my luggage? (荷物 = "luggage/baggage"; を = object marker; 預かって = te-form of "to hold/keep"; いただけますか = "could I receive the favor?")
- 禁煙室をお願いします: I'd like a non-smoking room, please (禁煙室 = "non-smoking room"; を = object marker; お願いします = "please/I request")
- Wi-Fiのパスワードを教えてください: Please tell me the Wi-Fi password (Wi-Fi = "Wi-Fi"; の = possessive particle; パスワード = "password"; を = object marker; 教えてください = "please tell/teach")
- 延泊は可能ですか: Is it possible to extend my stay? (延泊 = "extended stay/staying extra nights"; は = topic marker; 可能 = "possible"; です = copula; か = question marker)
</phrasebank>

## Dialogues

### Dialogue 1: Making a Hotel Reservation by Phone

A traveler calls a hotel in Kyoto to make a reservation for their upcoming trip.

<tts type="dialogue" speakers="Guest:Puck,Staff:Zephyr">
- Staff: はい、京都ホテルでございます。
- Guest: もしもし、予約をお願いしたいのですが。
- Staff: ありがとうございます。いつからいつまでのご予約でしょうか。
- Guest: 十二月十五日から二泊三日でお願いします。
- Staff: かしこまりました。お部屋のタイプはいかがなさいますか。
- Guest: ダブルルームで禁煙室をお願いします。
- Staff: かしこまりました。お名前をお願いできますか。
- Guest: 田中太郎です。
- Staff: 田中様、ありがとうございます。ご連絡先のお電話番号もお願いいたします。
- Guest: 090-1234-5678です。
- Staff: ありがとうございます。それでは、十二月十五日から二泊、ダブルルームの禁煙室でご予約を承りました。
</tts>

<dialogue_practice>
You are a hotel receptionist at a mid-range hotel in Japan. The user is a guest calling to make a reservation. Your role is to:
- Greet them professionally
- Ask for their desired dates (arrival and departure or nights)
- Inquire about room type preferences (single, double, twin, smoking/non-smoking)
- Request their name and contact information
- Confirm the reservation details

The user's goal is to successfully make a reservation using appropriate polite Japanese. Help them by responding naturally to their requests, asking clarifying questions if needed, and confirming details at the end.
</dialogue_practice>

### Dialogue 2: Checking into a Ryokan

A guest arrives at a traditional Japanese inn (ryokan) and goes through the check-in process.

<tts type="dialogue" speakers="Guest:Leda,Staff:Gacrux">
- Guest: こんにちは。チェックインをお願いします。
- Staff: いらっしゃいませ。ご予約のお名前をお伺いできますか。
- Guest: 山田花子です。
- Staff: 山田様、お待ちしておりました。二泊のご予約でございますね。
- Guest: はい、そうです。
- Staff: お部屋は二階の桜の間でございます。お夕食は六時から、朝食は七時半からとなっております。
- Guest: ありがとうございます。温泉は何時まで入れますか。
- Staff: 温泉は朝六時から夜十一時までご利用いただけます。
- Guest: わかりました。あと、明日の朝、駅までタクシーを呼んでいただけますか。
- Staff: かしこまりました。お時間はいかがいたしましょうか。
- Guest: 九時にお願いします。
- Staff: 承知いたしました。それでは、お部屋にご案内いたします。
</tts>

<dialogue_practice>
You are a staff member at a traditional ryokan. The user is a guest who has just arrived for check-in. Your role is to:
- Welcome them warmly
- Confirm their reservation name
- Inform them about their room, meal times, and onsen (hot spring) hours
- Answer any questions about facilities or services
- Offer to help with any special requests
- Guide them to their room

The user's goal is to check in smoothly, understand the ryokan's facilities and schedule, and make any necessary arrangements (like taxi reservations). Create a warm, traditional hospitality atmosphere.
</dialogue_practice>

### Dialogue 3: Reporting a Problem at an Accommodation

A guest discovers an issue with their room and contacts the front desk.

<tts type="dialogue" speakers="Guest:Puck,Staff:Zephyr">
- Guest: すみません、フロントですか。
- Staff: はい、フロントでございます。どうなさいましたか。
- Guest: 実は、部屋のエアコンが壊れているようなんです。
- Staff: 申し訳ございません。お部屋番号をお伺いできますか。
- Guest: 305号室です。
- Staff: 305号室の田中様ですね。大変申し訳ございません。すぐにスタッフを向かわせます。
- Guest: お願いします。あと、お湯も出ないんですが。
- Staff: お湯も出ませんか。重ねてお詫び申し上げます。よろしければ、お部屋を変更させていただきますが、いかがでしょうか。
- Guest: はい、お願いします。
- Staff: かしこまりました。すぐに別のお部屋をご用意いたします。ご不便をおかけして申し訳ございません。
</tts>

<dialogue_practice>
You are a hotel front desk staff member. The user is a guest calling from their room to report problems. Your role is to:
- Answer professionally and ask what the problem is
- Listen to their concerns (broken appliances, cleanliness issues, noise, etc.)
- Apologize sincerely for the inconvenience
- Offer solutions (send maintenance, change rooms, provide alternatives)
- Confirm their room number and name if needed
- Take immediate action to resolve the issue

The user's goal is to report problems clearly and get them resolved. They should practice describing various issues and accepting or negotiating solutions.
</dialogue_practice>

### Dialogue 4: Asking for Help with Transportation

A guest needs assistance getting to their next destination.

<tts type="dialogue" speakers="Guest:Leda,Staff:Sadaltager">
- Guest: すみません、ちょっとお聞きしたいことがあるんですが。
- Staff: はい、何でしょうか。
- Guest: 明日、嵐山に行きたいんですが、行き方を教えていただけますか。
- Staff: 嵐山でございますね。こちらから京都駅まで地下鉄で行きまして、そこからJR嵯峨野線に乗り換えていただきます。
- Guest: どのくらい時間がかかりますか。
- Staff: 全部で四十分ぐらいですね。
- Guest: なるほど。あと、大きな荷物があるので、タクシーで行った方がいいかもしれません。
- Staff: タクシーでしたら、一時間ほどかかりますが、直接行けますので楽だと思います。お呼びいたしましょうか。
- Guest: はい、お願いします。九時頃にお願いできますか。
- Staff: かしこまりました。九時にタクシーをご用意いたします。
</tts>

<dialogue_practice>
You are a hotel concierge or front desk staff member. The user is a guest who needs help with transportation and directions. Your role is to:
- Provide clear directions to various destinations (train, bus, walking)
- Suggest the best transportation options based on their needs
- Offer to call taxis or arrange transportation
- Give time estimates and cost information if possible
- Draw simple maps or write down instructions if needed
- Make reservations or arrangements on their behalf

The user's goal is to understand how to get to their destination and arrange appropriate transportation. They should practice asking about different places and transportation methods.
</dialogue_practice>

## Conversation

### Dialogue 1: Phone Reservation Roleplay

**Setup:** Student is planning a trip to Japan and needs to make a hotel reservation by phone. Teacher plays the hotel staff.

**Student role:** Call the hotel to make a reservation. Include:
- Desired dates (specific dates or "X nights from Y date")
- Number of people
- Room type preference (single/double/twin, smoking/non-smoking)
- Any special requests

**Teacher role:** Take the reservation professionally. Ask for:
- Name
- Contact information
- Confirm all details

**Objective:** Student practices making polite requests and providing information clearly. Teacher assesses student's ability to handle a structured service interaction.

### Dialogue 2: Problem Resolution at the Hotel

**Setup:** Student has checked into their hotel room and discovered several issues. They call the front desk to report problems.

**Student role:** Report 2-3 problems with your room (choose from: broken air conditioner, no hot water, noisy, dirty, wrong room type, missing amenities). Request solutions.

**Teacher role:** Respond as front desk staff. Apologize appropriately, ask clarifying questions, and offer solutions (send maintenance, change rooms, provide missing items).

**Objective:** Student practices describing problems, expressing dissatisfaction politely, and negotiating solutions. Teacher helps student use appropriate problem-reporting language and accept/negotiate remedies.

### Dialogue 3: Concierge Assistance

**Setup:** Student is staying at a hotel and needs help planning their day trip or getting to various destinations.

**Student role:** Ask the hotel concierge (teacher) for:
- Directions to a tourist site or destination
- Best transportation method (train/bus/taxi/walking)
- Time estimates and costs
- Request help booking a taxi or writing down directions

**Teacher role:** Provide helpful information as a concierge. Suggest options, explain routes, offer to make arrangements. Use authentic details about Japanese cities if possible.

**Objective:** Student practices asking for information, understanding directions, and requesting services. Teacher assesses comprehension and helps with transportation-related vocabulary and expressions.
