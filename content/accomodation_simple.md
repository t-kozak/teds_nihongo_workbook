# Lesson overview

In this lesson, you will learn how to communicate effectively in various accommodation-related situations in Japan. We'll cover making and modifying reservations, the check-in and check-out process, reporting problems with your room, and asking for assistance with transportation and directions. The lesson focuses on practical, everyday language that you can use immediately when traveling in Japan.

# Preparation

## Grammar Explanation

This lesson introduces several important grammatical structures that are essential for polite communication in accommodation settings:

**1. ～ています (Present continuous/State)**
This form indicates an ongoing state or that something has been done and the result continues. For reservations, we use it to say "I have a reservation":
- 予約しています (I have a reservation - literally "am in a state of having reserved")

**2. ～たいです (Want to do something)**
Used to express desire to do something in a polite way:
- 予約したいです (I want to make a reservation)
- チェックインしたいです (I want to check in)

**3. ～をお願いします (Please give me / I'd like to request)**
A polite way to request something or ask for a service:
- チェックアウトをお願いします (I'd like to check out, please)
- 領収書をお願いします (Receipt, please)

**4. ～てもらえますか (Could you do X for me?)**
This structure uses the potential form of もらう (to receive) to politely ask someone to do something for your benefit:
- タクシーを呼んでもらえますか (Could you call a taxi for me?)
- 荷物を預かってもらえますか (Could you hold my luggage for me?)

**5. ～が壊れています / ～が動きません (Something is broken / doesn't work)**
For reporting problems:
- エアコンが壊れています (The air conditioner is broken)
- シャワーが出ません (The shower doesn't work)

**6. ～への行き方 (How to get to ~)**
Used when asking for directions:
- 駅への行き方を教えてください (Please tell me how to get to the station)

## Useful Phrases

<phrasebank>
- 予約しています: I have a reservation (予約 - reservation; しています - am in a state of having done, indicating the reservation exists)
- 部屋を予約したいです: I'd like to book a room (部屋 - room; を - object marker; 予約 - reservation/to reserve; したい - want to do; です - polite ending)
- 名前は田中です: My name is Tanaka (名前 - name; は - topic marker; です - copula "is")
- チェックインをお願いします: I'd like to check in, please (チェックイン - check-in; を - object marker; お願いします - please/I request)
- 鍵をお願いします: The key, please (鍵 - key; を - object marker; お願いします - please/I request)
- 部屋は何階ですか: What floor is the room on? (部屋 - room; は - topic marker; 何階 - what floor; ですか - polite question marker)
- 朝食は何時からですか: What time does breakfast start? (朝食 - breakfast; は - topic marker; 何時から - from what time; ですか - question marker)
- エアコンが壊れています: The air conditioner is broken (エアコン - air conditioner; が - subject marker; 壊れています - is in a broken state)
- お湯が出ません: Hot water doesn't come out (お湯 - hot water; が - subject marker; 出ません - doesn't come out)

- タクシーを呼んでもらえますか: Could you call a taxi for me? (タクシー - taxi; を - object marker; 呼んで - call (te-form); もらえますか - can I receive the favor of/could you do for me)

## Dialogues

### Dialogue 1: Making a Phone Reservation

A traveler calls a hotel to make a reservation.

<tts type="dialogue" speakers="Traveler:Zephyr,Staff:Puck">
- Staff: はい、ホテルさくらでございます。
- Traveler: すみません、部屋を予約したいんですが。
- Staff: ありがとうございます。いつからいつまでですか。
- Traveler: 十二月十日から十二日まで、二泊です。
- Staff: かしこまりました。シングルですか、ダブルですか。
- Traveler: ダブルルームを一部屋お願いします。
- Staff: はい、お名前をお願いします。
- Traveler: 田中太郎です。
- Staff: ありがとうございます。お電話番号もお願いします。
- Traveler: 090-1234-5678です。
- Staff: かしこまりました。ご予約を承りました。
</tts>

<dialogue_practice>
You are a hotel receptionist at Hotel Sakura. The user is calling to make a reservation. Your role is to:
1. Greet them professionally with "はい、ホテルさくらでございます"
2. Ask for the dates of their stay (いつからいつまでですか)
3. Ask about room type (シングルですか、ダブルですか)
4. Request their name and phone number
5. Confirm the reservation

The user's goal is to successfully book a room at your hotel using polite Japanese. Be patient and help them practice the reservation process. If they struggle, you can prompt them gently in Japanese.
</dialogue_practice>

### Dialogue 2: Checking In at a Ryokan

A guest arrives at a traditional Japanese inn.

<tts type="dialogue" speakers="Guest:Leda,Staff:Gacrux">
- Staff: いらっしゃいませ。ご予約はされていますか。
- Guest: はい、予約しています。名前は鈴木です。
- Staff: 鈴木様ですね。少々お待ちください。はい、ございました。二泊のご予約ですね。
- Guest: はい、そうです。
- Staff: こちらが鍵でございます。お部屋は二階の二百一号室です。
- Guest: ありがとうございます。朝食は何時からですか。
- Staff: 朝食は七時から九時までです。一階の食堂でお召し上がりいただけます。
- Guest: わかりました。ありがとうございます。
- Staff: お荷物をお部屋までお持ちします。どうぞこちらへ。
</tts>

<dialogue_practice>
You are a guest checking in at a traditional ryokan. The user is playing the role of the ryokan staff member. Your goals are to:
1. Confirm you have a reservation (give your name as 鈴木)
2. Ask what time breakfast is served
3. Ask about any other facilities you're interested in

The staff member should greet you, confirm your reservation, provide your room key and number, and answer your questions. Practice using polite language appropriate for a ryokan setting.
</dialogue_practice>

### Dialogue 3: Reporting a Problem

A guest reports an issue with their room.

<tts type="dialogue" speakers="Guest:Puck,Staff:Zephyr">
- Guest: すみません、ちょっと問題があります。
- Staff: はい、どうされましたか。
- Guest: 部屋のエアコンが動きません。
- Staff: 申し訳ございません。すぐに確認いたします。お部屋は何号室ですか。
- Guest: 三百五号室です。
- Staff: かしこまりました。今すぐスタッフを向かわせます。
- Guest: お願いします。それから、お湯も出ないんですが。
- Staff: 大変申し訳ございません。併せて確認させていただきます。
- Guest: よろしくお願いします。
- Staff: ご不便をおかけして申し訳ございません。
</tts>

<dialogue_practice>
You are staying at a hotel and have encountered problems with your room. The user is the hotel staff. Your role is to:
1. Politely explain that there's a problem (問題があります)
2. Describe specific issues (the air conditioner doesn't work, hot water doesn't come out, etc.)
3. Provide your room number when asked

The staff member should apologize, ask for details, and arrange to fix the problems. Practice expressing problems politely and clearly in Japanese.
</dialogue_practice>

### Dialogue 4: Asking for Assistance at Check-out

A guest checks out and asks for help with transportation.

<tts type="dialogue" speakers="Guest:Zephyr,Staff:Sadaltager">
- Guest: チェックアウトをお願いします。
- Staff: かしこまりました。お部屋番号をお願いします。

- Staff: はい、少々お待ちください。こちらが明細書でございます。
- Guest: 領収書もお願いします。
- Staff: はい、こちらです。
- Guest: ありがとうございます。あのう、駅までタクシーを呼んでもらえますか。
- Staff: かしこまりました。駅までですね。五分ほどで到着いたします。
- Guest: お願いします。あと、荷物を少し預かってもらえますか。
- Staff: はい、もちろんです。こちらでお預かりいたします。
- Guest: ありがとうございます。三時ごろ取りに来ます。
- Staff: かしこまりました。お気をつけて。
</tts>

<dialogue_practice>
You are checking out of a hotel and need help with your onward journey. The user is the hotel staff. Your goals are to:
1. Complete the check-out process
2. Request a receipt (領収書)
3. Ask staff to call a taxi to the station
4. Ask if you can leave your luggage for a few hours

The staff member should process your check-out efficiently and help with your requests. Practice using ～てもらえますか structure for polite requests.
</dialogue_practice>

## Conversation

### Dialogue 1: Phone Reservation Role-play

**Student role:** You want to book a room at a hotel in Osaka for your upcoming trip. You need a twin room for 3 nights, checking in on November 20th. You're traveling with a friend.

**Teacher role:** You are the hotel receptionist. Answer the phone professionally, ask for necessary details (dates, room type, name, contact number), and confirm the reservation. If the student struggles, provide gentle prompts.

**Objectives:**
- Student: Successfully communicate all reservation details using polite Japanese
- Teacher: Assess the student's ability to handle a phone conversation and use of polite forms

### Dialogue 2: Check-in with Complications

**Student role:** You're checking in at your hotel, but you have some special requests. You have a reservation under your name. You need to ask: (1) if late check-out is available tomorrow, (2) what time breakfast ends, and (3) if they can recommend a nearby restaurant.

**Teacher role:** You are the front desk staff. Process the check-in, answer questions about late check-out (available for an extra fee), breakfast times, and recommend a restaurant. Use this opportunity to naturally introduce related vocabulary.

**Objectives:**
- Student: Practice checking in and making polite requests beyond the basic transaction
- Teacher: Introduce additional vocabulary and assess comprehension of responses

### Dialogue 3: Problem Resolution and Check-out

**Student role:** It's your last day. This morning you discovered that the toilet in your room isn't flushing properly. You need to report this problem, then proceed with check-out. You also need a taxi to the airport and want to know if you can pay by credit card.

**Teacher role:** You are the hotel staff. Apologize for the problem, arrange to have it fixed, process the check-out, confirm credit card payment is accepted, and call a taxi. Ask follow-up questions to make the conversation more natural (which airport, what time is the flight, etc.).

**Objectives:**
- Student: Practice problem reporting, check-out procedures, and making requests
- Teacher: Evaluate student's ability to handle unexpected situations and multi-step transactions
