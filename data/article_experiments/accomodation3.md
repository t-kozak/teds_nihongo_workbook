# Lesson overview

This lesson covers the essential Japanese language skills needed for navigating accommodations in Japan. You will learn how to make and modify reservations, handle the check-in and check-out processes, report issues with your room, and ask for common services like calling a taxi or getting directions. The phrases and vocabulary are applicable to hotels, traditional inns (ryokan), and AirBnBs.

# Preparation

## Grammar Explanation

To handle accommodation situations effectively, we'll focus on a few key grammatical patterns for making polite requests and stating needs.

### Making Requests: ～をお願いします (～ o onegai shimasu)

This is a versatile and polite way to ask for a service or an item. You simply take the noun for what you want and add 「をお願いします」.

*   チェックインをお願いします。(Chekku-in o onegai shimasu.) - I'd like to check in, please.
*   領収書をお願いします。(Ryōshūsho o onegai shimasu.) - I'd like a receipt, please.

### Expressing Desires: ～たいです (～ tai desu)

To say you "want to do" something, you take the stem of a verb (the part before `-masu`) and add `-tai desu`.

*   予約し**たい**です。(Yoyaku shi**tai** desu.) - I want to make a reservation. (from します, shimasu)
*   荷物を預け**たい**です。(Nimotsu o azuke**tai** desu.) - I want to leave my luggage. (from 預けます, azukemasu)

### Asking for Permission/Possibility: ～てもいいですか (～ te mo ii desu ka) & ～できますか (～ dekimasu ka)

To politely ask "May I...?" or "Is it okay if...?", use the te-form of a verb followed by 「もいいですか」.

*   荷物をここに置い**てもいいですか**。(Nimotsu o koko ni oi**te mo ii desu ka**.) - May I put my luggage here?

To ask "Is it possible to...?" or "Can I...?", use a noun or verb in dictionary form followed by 「こと」 and then 「ができますか」. For simple nouns, you can often just use 「[Noun] はできますか」.

*   予約の変更は**できますか**。(Yoyaku no henkō wa **dekimasu ka**.) - Is it possible to change the reservation?
*   カードで払うことは**できますか**。(Kādo de harau koto wa **dekimasu ka**.) - Is it possible to pay by card?

### Polite Requests for Action: ～ていただけますか (～ te itadakemasu ka)

This is a very polite way to ask someone to do something for you, equivalent to "Could you please...?". It's more formal than `～てください` (～ te kudasai). You use the te-form of the verb.

*   タクシーを呼ん**でいただけますか**。(Takushī o yon**de itadakemasu ka**.) - Could you please call a taxi for me?
*   駅までの行き方を教え**ていただけますか**。(Eki made no ikikata o oshie**te itadakemasu ka**.) - Could you please teach me the way to the station?

## Useful Phrases

<phrasebank>
- 予約をしたいのですが: I would like to make a reservation. (予約 (yoyaku) means "reservation". を (o) is the object marker. したい (shitai) is the "want to do" form of the verb する (suru), to do. のですが (no desu ga) is a polite and soft way to introduce a topic or request.)
- チェックインをお願いします: I'd like to check in, please. (チェックイン (chekku-in) is the English "check-in". を (o) is the object marker. お願いします (onegai shimasu) is a polite phrase for making a request, literally "I request".)
- 禁煙の部屋は空いていますか: Do you have any non-smoking rooms available? (禁煙 (kin'en) means "non-smoking". の (no) is the possessive particle. 部屋 (heya) is "room". は (wa) is the topic marker. 空いていますか (aiteimasu ka) is the polite question form of the verb 空く (aku), to become empty or available.)
- 予約の変更はできますか: Is it possible to change my reservation? (予約 (yoyaku) is "reservation". の (no) is a particle indicating possession or connection. 変更 (henkō) means "change". は (wa) marks the topic. できますか (dekimasu ka) is a polite way to ask "is it possible?" or "can you do it?".)
- エアコンが壊れているようです: It seems the air conditioner is broken. (エアコン (eakon) is "air conditioner". が (ga) is the subject marker. 壊れている (kowareteiru) is the state of being broken, from the verb 壊れる (kowareru), to break. ようです (yō desu) means "it seems like" or "it looks like", used to state an observation gently.)
- タオルをもう一枚もらえませんか: Could I get another towel? (タオル (taoru) is "towel". を (o) marks the object. もう一枚 (mō ichimai) means "one more flat object" - 一枚 is the counter for flat things. もらえませんか (moraemasen ka) is a polite request, literally "could I not receive?", from the potential form of もらう (morau), to receive.)
- タクシーを呼んでいただけますか: Could you please call a taxi for me? (タクシー (takushī) is "taxi". を (o) is the object marker. 呼んで (yonde) is the te-form of the verb 呼ぶ (yobu), to call. いただけますか (itadakemasu ka) is a very polite way to ask someone to do something for your benefit.)
- チェックアウトは何時ですか: What time is check-out? (チェックアウト (chekku-auto) is "check-out". は (wa) is the topic marker. 何時ですか (nanji desu ka) means "what time is it?".)
</phrasebank>

## Dialogues

### Dialogue 1: Making a Reservation

A traveler is calling a ryokan (traditional Japanese inn) to book a room.
<tts type="dialogue" speakers="Staff:Takashi,Guest:Keiko">
- Staff: はい、旅館「さくら」でございます。
- Guest: もしもし、予約をしたいのですが。来週の金曜日から一泊、大人二人で泊まれますか。
- Staff: 来週の金曜日、大人二名様ですね。少々お待ちください...はい、空いております。
- Guest: よかった。禁煙の部屋をお願いします。
- Staff: かしこまりました。では、お名前とお電話番号をいただけますでしょうか。
- Guest: はい、名前はミラーです。電話番号は...
</tts>
<dialogue_practice>
You are the guest calling a Japanese inn (ryokan) to make a reservation. I will be the ryokan staff. Your goal is to successfully book a room for a specific date and number of people. You should also make a special request, such as asking for a non-smoking room or a room with a view. Start by telling me you'd like to make a reservation.
</dialogue_practice>

### Dialogue 2: Checking In and Asking for Advice

A tourist is checking into their hotel and asks the receptionist for directions.
<tts type="dialogue" speakers="Receptionist:Haruka,Tourist:David">
- Receptionist: いらっしゃいませ。
- Tourist: こんにちは。チェックインをお願いします。予約したミラーです。
- Receptionist: ミラー様ですね。パスポートを拝見してもよろしいでしょうか。
- Tourist: はい、どうぞ。すみません、この近くにおすすめのレストランはありますか。
- Receptionist: はい、ございます。この地図に印をつけますね。当ホテルから歩いて5分ほどです。
- Tourist: ありがとうございます。助かります。
</tts>
<dialogue_practice>
You are a tourist arriving at your hotel in Japan. I will be the hotel receptionist. Your first goal is to check in. You should state your name. After you've shown me your passport, your second goal is to ask for a recommendation, like a good restaurant or a nearby tourist spot, and ask for directions. Start by saying hello and telling me you'd like to check in.
</dialogue_practice>

## Conversation

### Dialogue 1

*   **Situation:** The student is a tourist trying to resolve an issue at their hotel. The teacher is the hotel front desk staff.
*   **Student's Role:** You have just returned to your hotel room to find that your key card doesn't work. You need to go to the front desk to explain the problem.
*   **Student's Objective:** Politely explain the situation to the staff and get a new key card that works.
*   **Teacher's Role:** You are the front desk staff. Listen to the guest's problem, apologize for the inconvenience, and issue a new key card.

### Dialogue 2

*   **Situation:** The student is checking out of an Airbnb. The teacher is the Airbnb host.
*   **Student's Role:** You are ready to check out. You need to confirm the check-out procedure with the host. You also want to ask if you can leave your luggage for a few hours after checking out.
*   **Student's Objective:** Successfully confirm the check-out time and procedure, and get permission to store your luggage until the afternoon.
*   **Teacher's Role:** You are the Airbnb host. Confirm the check-out time is 11 AM. Explain that the guest should leave the key on the table. Agree to let them leave their luggage in the entryway until 3 PM.
