Navigating Public Transport in Japan

Context/Summary

This lesson focuses on the essential Japanese phrases and vocabulary needed to navigate Japan's extensive public transportation system. You'll learn how to buy tickets at train stations, ask about train schedules and platforms, inquire about routes between destinations, and communicate effectively when using trains, buses, and ferries. The lesson covers common scenarios travelers and residents encounter daily, from asking "Which train goes to Tokyo?" to "When is the next bus?" You'll also learn how to ask about transfers between different types of transport and understand key station announcements.

Grammar Explanation

Particles for Transportation

〜で (de) - Indicates the means or method of transportation - バスで来ましたa-furigana="でんしゃ">電車で行きます (densha de ikimasu) - "go by train" - バスで来ました (basu de kimashita) - "came by bus"

〜に乗る (ni noru) - "to ride/take" (a vehicle) - バスに乗る data-furigana="でんしゃ">電車に乗る (densha ni noru) - "take the train" - バスに乗る (basu ni noru) - "take the bus"

〜から〜まで (kara...made) - "from...to/until" - 何時から何時まできょう">東京から大阪まで (Tōkyō kara Ōsaka made) - "from Tokyo to Osaka" - 何時から何時まで (nanji kara nanji made) - "from what time to what time"

Question Patterns

どの〜 (dono~) - "which" (when choosing from options) - どのホーム"which">どの電車 (dono densha) - "which train" - どのホーム (dono hōmu) - "which platform"

何番線 (nanban-sen) - "what track/platform number" - Used when asking about platform numbers at stations

〜まではどのくらいかかりますか (made donokurai kakarimasu ka) - "How long does it take to~?" - 東京までどのくらいかかりますか - "How long does it take to Tokyo?"

〜は何処ですか (wa doko desu ka) - "Where is~?" - チケット売り場はどこですか - "Where is the ticket office?"

〜てもいいですか (te mo ii desu ka) - "May I~?" / "Is it okay to~?" - クレジットカードで払い(て)もいいですか - "May I pay by credit card?"

Time Expressions

〜分後 (fun-go) - "in ~ minutes" - 五分後 (go-fun-go) - "in 5 minutes"

次の〜 (tsugi no~) - "the next~" - 次の駅" data-furigana="つぎ">次の電車 (tsugi no densha) - "the next train" - 次の駅 (tsugi no eki) - "the next station"

Short Story

今日、私は初めて一人で東京から京都まで新幹線で行きました。東京駅はとても大きくて、初始は少し心配でした。駅員さんに「京都行きの新幹線は何番線ですか」と聞きました。駅員さんは親切に「十四番線です。次の電車は十五分後に出ますよ」と教えてくれました。

チケット売り場で"京都まで大人一枚をお願いします"と言って切符を買いました。ホームに着いて電光掲示板を見ました。「次は新大阪、京都です」と書いてありました。新幹線に乗って窓から富士山が見えました。とても美しかったです。京都に着くまで二時間半かかりましたが快適な旅でした。京都駅で地下鉄に乗り換えてホテルまで行きました。日本の公共交通機関は本当に便利ですね。

Translation: Today, I went from Tokyo to Kyoto by Shinkansen alone for the first time. Tokyo Station is very large, so I was a bit worried at first. I asked a station attendant, "Which platform is the Shinkansen bound for Kyoto?" The attendant kindly told me, "It's platform 14. The next train leaves in 15 minutes."

At the ticket office, I said "One adult ticket to Kyoto please" and bought my ticket. When I arrived at the platform, I looked at the electronic display board. It said "Next stops: Shin-Osaka, Kyoto." I boarded the Shinkansen and saw Mt. Fuji from the window. It was very beautiful. It took two and a half hours to reach Kyoto, but it was a comfortable journey. At Kyoto Station, I transferred to the subway and went to my hotel. Japan's public transportation really is convenient.
