Restaurant Japanese: Ordering, Reservations, and Dining Out

Context/Summary

This lesson focuses on practical Japanese for restaurant situations. You'll learn how to make and modify reservations, order food using demonstrative pronouns (これ、それ、あれ), discuss various types of dishes and cuisines, and ask important questions about your food—particularly regarding spice levels. Whether you're dining at a casual eatery or a formal restaurant, these phrases will help you navigate the experience with confidence.

Grammar Explanation

Demonstrative Pronouns: これ、それ、あれ

In Japanese, demonstrative pronouns help you point out specific items without knowing their exact names—perfect for ordering food!

  • これ (kore) - "this" - Used for items close to the speaker
  • それ (sore) - "that" - Used for items close to the listener or at medium distance
  • あれ (are) - "that over there" - Used for items far from both speaker and listener

When ordering at a restaurant: - それをお願しますpan>をください (kore wo kudasai) - "This, please" - それをお願いします (sore wo onegaishimasu) - "That, please" (more polite)

Counters for Food

Japanese uses different counter words depending on what you're counting. For food:

  • 〜人前 (〜ninmae) - portions/servings (e.g., 二人前 - two servings)
  • 〜つ (〜tsu) - general counter for items (e.g., 三つ - three items)
  • &〜;個 (〜ko) - counter for small objects (e.g., 二個 - two pieces)
  • 〜杯 (〜hai) - counter for cups/bowls/glasses (e.g., 一杯 - one cup)

Politeness Levels

Restaurants typically use polite Japanese. Key polite forms include:

  • 〜てください (〜te kudasai) - "Please do..."
  • 〜をお願いします (〜wo onegaishimasu) - "I'd like.../Please give me..."
  • 〜ていただけますか (〜te itadakemasuka) - "Could you please...?" (very polite)
